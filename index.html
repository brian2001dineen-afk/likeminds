About us

  • Search for groups – find a group looking for members. Sort by given topics on the site, or search by keyword or book name.
  • Apply for a group – introduce yourself and convince the organizer why you would make a good fit for the team.
  • Propose a group – create a group page advertising your chosen material, schedule, and meeting frequency. The page may also contain a description outlining more specific details as to the group's proposed facilitation.
  • Manage your group – assign roles to members and oversee your team's progress. Easily check what you need to prioritize and where people might be falling behind.

Decide your workmode

  1. Open choice – material is switched out as the group completes it. There is no one subject of study.
    • This is useful for more casual groups, who want to learn many different things in a relaxed setting.
    • Can be difficult to constantly curate interesting topics at a fixed pace.
  2. Commitment – a singular book or source of reference is chosen. Any extra material should correlate to the given topic in some way.
